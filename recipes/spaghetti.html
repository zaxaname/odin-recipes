Classic Spaghetti Recipe

Spaghetti, a beloved staple in Italian cuisine, finds its origins in Southern Italy, particularly Naples, where pasta-making became an art form centuries ago. Derived from the Italian word "spago," meaning "string" or "twine," spaghetti is a long, thin pasta crafted from durum wheat semolina and water. Historically, the process of creating this pasta involved hand-rolling dough into thin strands, a practice that evolved with technological advancements. Its popularity soared in the late 19th century when pasta-making machines streamlined production, making spaghetti accessible to a broader audience. This simple yet versatile pasta became synonymous with Italian culinary heritage and eventually spread across the globe as a beloved comfort food.

Creating a classic spaghetti dish is a straightforward and comforting process. Begin by boiling spaghetti pasta until it reaches the desired tenderness. In a skillet, sauté minced garlic in olive oil until fragrant. Combine crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, basil, red pepper flakes for a kick (if desired), salt, and pepper, simmering the sauce for a brief period. Once the pasta is cooked, toss it in the sauce until evenly coated. Finish by garnishing with fresh basil leaves and serving hot, optionally accompanied by grated Parmesan cheese for added flavor. This straightforward recipe captures the essence of Italian simplicity and flavors, making it a beloved favorite for countless pasta enthusiasts.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti pasta
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss until the pasta is well coated.
  5. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh basil leaves.
  6. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
