Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna, a quintessential Italian dish, boasts a history as rich as its layers. Originating from ancient Greece, this pasta dish evolved over centuries, eventually finding its place in Italian cuisine. Its name, derived from the Greek word "lasanon," refers to the pot used to cook it. Early renditions involved layers of pasta dough with meat fillings, baked to perfection. As it journeyed through Italy, different regions embraced variations, adding diverse ingredients like cheeses, vegetables, and assorted sauces. Today, lasagna remains a beloved symbol of Italian culinary tradition, celebrated worldwide for its comforting, layered charm.

Now, as for the recipe itself, making lasagna is a delightful process that begins with crafting a robust meat sauce. Sauteed Italian sausage, onions, garlic, and a medley of tomatoes simmer together to create a flavorful base. Meanwhile, lasagna noodles cook to a perfect al dente. Assembling the dish involves layering the meat sauce with the noodles, creamy ricotta cheese, slices of mozzarella, and a generous sprinkle of Parmesan. Repeat these layers in a baking dish until it's brimming with deliciousness. Baked in the oven until golden and bubbly, the result is an irresistible amalgamation of textures and flavors, inviting everyone to savor each comforting, cheesy bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cans (6 oz each) tomato paste
  • 2 cans (6.5 oz each) canned tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons dried basil leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 pound mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the sausage, onion, and garlic over medium heat until the sausage is browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce, water, sugar, basil, Italian seasoning, parsley, salt, and pepper. Simmer, covered, for about 1 1/2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. In a separate pot, cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain.
  4. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese with egg, salt, and set aside.
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. To assemble, spread a layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange a layer of noodles over the meat sauce. Spread a layer of ricotta cheese mixture. Place a layer of mozzarella cheese slices. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, ending with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  7. Cover with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  8. Let it cool for 15 minutes before serving.
